I'm Arabella. I read cards, tea leaves, dice, books and bones, and I've been at it for over a decade. Most of my working time now goes to archives and primary sources on regional folk practice. Juniper Divination is where that research lives.

## How I Work

Most of what's on this site is sourced from historical practice: Irish folklore, Celtic Reconstructionist material, nineteenth-century cartomancy manuals, tea-leaf reading traditions, ogham, bibliomancy, chaos magic. Where I can, I name the source, say where a practice actually comes from, and note what changed along the way.

That matters because a lot of what gets taught as "traditional" online isn't. I'd rather tell you where something comes from and let you decide what to do with it than dress an invention up as an inheritance.

I don't work for a platform or a publisher. The site stays the way I want it because readers keep it funded directly.

## One Note on Scope

Divination, at least the way I teach it, is a tool for thinking clearly about your own life not a fixed prediction of what will happen. Readings are not a substitute for professional help. I'm not a doctor, lawyer, or financial advisor. What I offer is a way to sit with a question, look at it from a few angles, and decide what to do next.
