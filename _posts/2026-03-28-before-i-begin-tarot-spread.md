The Before I Begin spread is for the moment before you commit to something. Four cards: where you're starting from emotionally, what to be careful of, what you're already bringing to this, and what going well actually looks like. No ritual prep, no special deck, no prior experience needed.

## When to Use This Spread

Use it at the start of something real. A project, a decision you've been circling, a new habit, a season you want to approach differently.

It also works well when you feel like you're on the edge of something but can't name what's holding you. The spread won't tell you whether to proceed. It shows you the internal landscape you're bringing to that choice.

Skip it when you need immediate, concrete guidance on a specific situation. This spread isn't built for that. Use it before, not during.

## The Card Positions

**Card 1: The disposition of my heart — What stirs within you now.**

This card names your actual emotional starting state, not the one you'd prefer to have.

Disposition means your prevailing tendency right now — the emotional current running underneath everything else. It shifts over time, but knowing what it is today helps you work with it rather than around it.

Whatever comes up, don't reframe it into something more flattering. If you're anxious, this card will probably show that. If you're carrying grief into this new beginning, it'll show up here too. That's not a bad sign. It's just where you're starting from.

**Card 2: A shadow on the path ahead — Where to tread carefully.**

This card is dealt sideways. Read it upright or reversed, whichever feels right to you when you look at it.

This card isn't a prediction. It's a caution.

A shadow doesn't mean the path is blocked. It names something ahead that will need your attention, and you're better off knowing about it now.

This position usually surfaces a pattern rather than a specific event. A tendency to give up before things get hard. A fear that shows up reliably when things start going well. A blind spot in how you handle pressure. The shadow is generally familiar, even when you don't want to acknowledge it.

Read this card with curiosity. It's telling you where to keep your eyes open, not that something will go wrong.

**Card 3: A gift I carry within me — What you already hold.**

You're not starting from zero.

You have capacities that are already yours, built through everything that's come before this. This card names one of them.

The gift named here surprises people sometimes. It's often something you take so thoroughly for granted that you've stopped counting it, or something you've been dismissing as unreliable.

The Strength card points to resilience built through difficulty. The Two of Pentacles names a real skill for managing competing demands that you've been undervaluing. The High Priestess identifies intuition you've been second-guessing.

Take this position seriously. People tend to move past the strength cards quickly and linger on the difficult ones, but they deserve equal weight.

**Card 4: The bloom — What flourishing looks like.**

This card is also dealt sideways. Same as Card 2: read it upright or reversed based on what resonates when you turn it over.

This card shows you the specific shape of what going well looks like from here.

Not success in the abstract. The particular version of flourishing available to you from this starting point, with these gifts, given what you're watching out for.

Bloom is precise because it's specific. The Ten of Pentacles shows flourishing as long-term stability built gradually. The Ace of Cups points to emotional abundance — connection, creative opening, a shift in how available you are to the people around you. The World names a genuine completion, arriving somewhere you can recognize as a real destination.

Hold this card as an orientation, not a guarantee.

## Reading the Spread

Lay all four cards before you read any of them.

Look at Card 1 and Card 3 together first. Your emotional starting point and your already-present strengths are in relationship. Anxiety alongside patience reads differently than excitement alongside persistence. The combination tells you more than either card alone.

Then look at Card 2 and Card 4 together. What's the obstacle, and what's on the other side of it? These two cards often describe the path directly.

Read all four as a sequence last. Where you're starting from, what to watch for, what you have, and where that leads. It usually holds together as a single coherent picture.

## Tips for Beginners

Cards 2 and 4 are dealt sideways. When you turn them over, read them upright or reversed based on which orientation feels right. There's no rule here. Trust your first instinct.

If Card 3 surprises or confuses you, ask someone who knows you well what strength they'd name first. The things we've stopped seeing in ourselves are often what other people name immediately.

Write down your reading. A threshold moment is exactly when you'll want to look back later and see what you knew at the start.

Any deck works for this. A worn deck, an inexpensive deck, even a deck of regular playing cards (learn how to read those [here](https://ko-fi.com/s/95f9c29692)). The spread doesn't require a deck that matches the occasion.
